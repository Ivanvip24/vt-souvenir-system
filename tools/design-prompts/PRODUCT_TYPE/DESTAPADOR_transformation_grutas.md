# BOTTLE OPENER TRANSFORMATION - Grutas de Tolantongo
*Adapting existing square design to destapador format*

## TRANSFORMATION CONTEXT:
```
You are TRANSFORMING an existing design into a bottle opener format.
ORIGINAL DESIGN: A square illustration of Grutas de Tolantongo featuring:
- Central circular thermal pool with turquoise water
- Orange/red canyon rocks forming backdrop
- Small rustic house with red/orange roof
- Lush green trees framing the scene
- "GRUTAS de Tolantongo" text at bottom
- Gray stones at the base
```

## BOTTLE OPENER REQUIREMENTS:
```
NEW FORMAT: Vertical rounded rectangle bottle opener
- Dimensions: 3" wide × 6" tall (2:1 ratio)
- THREE VOIDS that will be laser cut out:
  * Top: Large circle (1.2" diameter) - bottle opening function
  * Middle: Two small circles (0.3" each) - mounting holes
- These voids are EMPTY SPACES - no design elements here
```

## TRANSFORMATION INSTRUCTIONS:

### STEP 1 - DECONSTRUCT THE ORIGINAL:
```
Take these elements from the original design:
- The circular thermal pool with turquoise water
- The orange/red canyon rock formation
- The small house with distinctive roof
- The green trees (multiple sizes)
- The text "GRUTAS de Tolantongo"
- The stone base elements
- The overall color palette
```

### STEP 2 - RECONSTRUCT FOR BOTTLE OPENER:

#### TOP SECTION (Working around large void):
```
- Take "GRUTAS" text and curve it ABOVE the bottle opener hole
- Place smaller trees on FAR LEFT and FAR RIGHT of the void
- Canyon peaks can rise behind the void area
- Keep this section lighter on details due to void
```

#### MIDDLE SECTION (Working around two small voids):
```
SPLIT THE THERMAL POOL:
- Instead of one central circular pool, create TWO curved pools
- Left pool: Curves around the left side of the left void
- Right pool: Curves around the right side of the right void
- Connect them visually with a waterfall or stream BETWEEN the voids
- Keep the same turquoise water color and styling

POSITION THE HOUSE:
- Move the house slightly down and to one side
- Scale it smaller to fit the narrower format
- Keep its distinctive roof and window details
```

#### BOTTOM SECTION (Full width available):
```
- "de Tolantongo" text prominently placed
- Larger trees can be positioned here
- Stone base elements spread across bottom
- This area has the most design freedom
```

### STEP 3 - ADAPT ELEMENTS FOR VERTICAL FORMAT:

#### CANYON TRANSFORMATION:
```
Original: Wide backdrop behind pool
New: VERTICAL canyon walls on left and right sides
- Stretch the rock formations vertically
- Create dramatic height instead of width
- Keep the orange/red gradient coloring
- Add more vertical striations and layers
```

#### TREE REDISTRIBUTION:
```
Original: Trees on both sides of square
New: Strategic placement avoiding voids
- Tall trees as vertical frame elements
- Smaller trees filling gaps between main elements
- Use trees to "mask" the edges of void spaces
- Maintain the same lush green coloring
```

#### POOL REIMAGINING:
```
Original: One central circular pool
New: Terraced pools flowing vertically
- Top pools (around top void): Smaller, decorative
- Middle pools (around middle voids): Split design
- Bottom pools: Can be larger and more prominent
- Add vertical waterfalls connecting levels
```

```
MUST PRESERVE:
```


## COMPOSITION STRATEGY:

### VERTICAL FLOW DESIGN:
```
Instead of centered circular composition:
1. Create vertical CASCADE effect
2. Elements flow from top to bottom
3. Use the voids as "breaks" in the flow
4. Water appears to flow around obstacles (the voids)
5. Maintain visual continuity despite gaps
```

### WORKING WITH NEGATIVE SPACE:
```
The voids become part of the design:
- Top void: Could be "where steam rises through"
- Middle voids: "Stepping stones" in the water flow
- Use surrounding elements to frame voids naturally
- Don't fight the voids - incorporate them
```

## FINAL DESIGN DESCRIPTION:

Transform the square Grutas de Tolantongo design into a vertical bottle opener format (3"×6"). Take the central circular pool and split it into terraced pools that flow around the functional voids. The text "GRUTAS" curves above the top bottle opener hole. The orange/red canyon rocks become dramatic vertical walls framing both sides. The rustic house is repositioned to the lower section, smaller but maintaining its character. Trees are strategically placed to avoid voids while creating a lush frame. "de Tolantongo" appears prominently at the bottom. The turquoise waters cascade vertically through the design, splitting around the two middle mounting holes like a natural stream around rocks. Maintain the exact color palette and semi-realistic style of the original, but reimagined for the vertical bottle opener format. The design should feel like the same paradise destination, just viewed through a different lens - vertical and flowing rather than centered and circular.
