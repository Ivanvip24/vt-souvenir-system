# AXKAN - Brand Identity & Decision System

AXKAN is not just a brand. It is a philosophy of Mexican cultural pride transformed into premium touristic souvenirs that create lasting emotional connections.

---

## Core Identity

### Brand Name Origin
**AXKAN** (pronounced ahsh-KAHN) is derived from Maya language meaning "ahora" / "now" / "the present moment." It represents the eternal present where past tradition and future vision converge.

### Tagline Hierarchy
- **Primary Tagline**: "Recuerdos Hechos Souvenir" (Memories Made Souvenir)
- **Emotional Tagline**: "Detonadores de Orgullo Mexicano" (Triggers of Mexican Pride)
- **Commercial Tagline**: "Llévate México Contigo" (Take Mexico With You)
- **Support Tagline**: "Souvenirs Que Sí Importan" (Souvenirs That Actually Matter)

### Brand Archetype
**The Magician** - Transforming traditions into treasures, transforming moments into eternal memories, transforming tourists into ambassadors of Mexican pride.

---

## Brand Philosophy

### The Mariachi Moment
AXKAN exists to capture and preserve the "mariachi moment" - that instant when a tourist hears mariachi music for the first time and feels tears forming, or when someone sees the Pyramids of Teotihuacán and feels connected to something ancient and profound. AXKAN's products are physical vessels that encapsulate these emotional peaks.

### Core Belief
Mexico's cultural richness deserves to be represented by premium, authentic products - not cheap, stereotypical souvenirs that diminish the culture they claim to celebrate. Every AXKAN product should make both the buyer and Mexico proud.

### Anti-Stereotype Commitment

**AXKAN explicitly rejects:**
- Sombreros and mustaches as primary cultural symbols
- Neon colors and gaudy designs
- Low-quality materials that break quickly
- Designs that mock or trivialize Mexican culture
- Generic "tourist trap" aesthetics

**AXKAN embraces:**
- Pre-Hispanic symbolism (Aztec, Maya, Zapotec)
- Regional authenticity and diversity
- Artisan craftsmanship quality
- Sophisticated color palettes rooted in tradition
- Designs that create genuine cultural connection

```

10% - ACCENT COLORS - supporting elements (distributed across palette)
```

### Logo Components

1. **Full Logo**: Jaguar mark + decorative "AXKAN" wordmark
2. **Jaguar Mark**: Stylized jaguar head with Mayan grecas patterns
3. **Simplified Jaguar**: Single-color jaguar mark (for small applications)
4. **1-Tinta Version**: Single color version for limited print

### The Jaguar Symbol
The jaguar incorporates:
- Pre-Hispanic geometric patterns (grecas) in cyan and pink
- Green leaf elements symbolizing growth
- Red facial markings representing tradition
- Orange outline creating warmth and energy

### The Decorative AXKAN Wordmark
Each letter contains cultural textures:
- A: Magenta with Mayan patterns
- X: Green with organic textures
- K: Orange with traditional motifs
- A: Turquoise with architectural elements
- N: Red with cultural symbols

**CRITICAL RULE: The decorative textured "AXKAN" wordmark is for LOGO USE ONLY.**

For destination names (YUCATAN, CANCUN, COZUMEL, etc.), use:
- Plain RL AQVA font
- Each letter a different solid brand color
- NO textures inside letters (that's reserved for the logo)

## Photography vs. Illustration

### The Integration Rule
**Photography and illustration can coexist, but illustration should FRAME or ACCENT photography, never compete with it.**

### When to Use PHOTOGRAPHY:
- Hero images of destinations (beaches, ruins, cities)
- Product photography showing actual souvenirs
- Lifestyle shots (people enjoying Mexico)
- Backgrounds for promotional materials
- Social media content featuring real places
- Realistic elements: flamingos, toucans, jaguars, palm trees, pyramids

### When to Use ILLUSTRATION:
- The jaguar logo (always)
- The AXKAN logotype (always)
- Pattern elements (flowers, spirals, leaves)
- Icons and UI elements
- Decorative frames and borders

### Photographic Treatments:

| Treatment | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Full color** | Hero product shots, social media |
| **Duotone (green or pink)** | Background textures, artistic effect |
| **Cutout/PNG** | Collage compositions, layered designs |
| **With diagonal slice** | Feature grids, promotional layouts |

### Layer Hierarchy for Mixed Compositions:
```
LAYER 1 (back): Photography
LAYER 2 (mid): Subtle pattern at 10-20% opacity
LAYER 3 (front): Logo, text, illustrated accents
```

### Integration Frameworks:

**1. Photo as Stage** - Photo is full background, product floats on top
**2. Diagonal Split** - Half photo / half brand color at 45°
**3. Photo Window** - Brand pattern frames a photo window
**4. Duotone Bridge** - Photo in brand color duotone, product in full color

## Los 5 Diferenciadores AXKAN

> **La Analogía**: Si la competencia es un "sello de goma" que estampa el mismo dibujo en todos lados, AXKAN es un pintor que viaja a cada pueblo para retratar su alma, pero con la velocidad y eficiencia de una impresora láser de última generación.

### 1. Diseño Auténtico vs. "Plantillas Genéricas"
La competencia usa un modelo "copiar y pegar" - mismo colibrí con diferente texto. AXKAN diseña específicamente para cada lugar, capturando la esencia única de cada región.

### 2. Conexión Emocional (El "Efecto Mariachi")
No vendemos un objeto de MDF; vendemos la sensación de orgullo de ser mexicano y la belleza de sus tradiciones.

### 3. Manufactura "A to Z" y Calidad Visual
Control total: diseño, corte láser, limpieza, armado y empacado. Producto "sumamente cuidado" con presentación excelente.

### 4. Mentalidad Tecnológica en un Mercado Artesanal
Automatización mediante sistemas (Claude Code, Notion) para gestionar pedidos, envíos y producción. Escalabilidad que la competencia no tiene.

### 5. Estrategia B2B Colaborativa
Alianzas con proveedores locales - ellos nos dicen qué es típico de la región, nosotros ponemos la magia del diseño profesional.

## Decision Framework

### The AXKAN Test

Apply to ANY brand decision:

1. **Does it trigger pride?** - Would a Mexican person feel proud seeing this?
2. **Is it culturally authentic?** - Is this rooted in real Mexican tradition?
3. **Is it premium?** - Would this feel at home in a museum gift shop?
4. **Is it accessible?** - Can a regular tourist afford this?
5. **Will it last?** - Will this be treasured for years?

**If any answer is "no," the decision fails the test.**

### Communication Decision Tree

**When someone asks about AXKAN products:**
→ Lead with emotional benefit (the memory they'll preserve)
→ Support with cultural context (the meaning behind the design)
→ Close with practical value (quality, durability, price)

**When handling objections:**
→ "Too expensive": Focus on cost-per-year and emotional value
→ "Found cheaper": Explain quality difference and cultural authenticity
→ "Need to think": Create gentle urgency, ask what specific concerns remain

**When representing the brand publicly:**
→ Always position as premium-accessible, never discount-oriented
→ Always lead with cultural story, never just product features
→ Always thank people for interest in Mexican culture

## Estrategias de Negociación y Ventas

### Principios Psicológicos Fundamentales

| Principio | Aplicación AXKAN |
|-----------|------------------|
| Reciprocidad | Dar valor primero (diseño gratis, info útil) |
| Anclaje | Presentar precio alto primero, luego el real |
| Aversión a la pérdida | Enfatizar lo que pierden, no lo que ganan |
| Prueba social | Mencionar otros clientes/hoteles similares |
| Escasez | Capacidad de producción limitada |
| Compromiso progresivo | Pequeños "sí" antes del grande |

### Key Sales Situations

**"¿Cuánto cuestan?"** → Don't give price immediately. Ask qualifying questions first, then present price with anchoring.

**"Está muy caro"** → Use Feel-Felt-Found + value reframe. Calculate cost-per-day of advertising (less than 1 cent for 5+ years).

**"Déjame pensarlo"** → Identify real objection (60% have unexpressed concerns). Create legitimate urgency with production capacity.

**"¿Me haces descuento?"** → NEVER give discount without trade-off. Offer more volume for better price, or add value instead.

**"Solo estoy cotizando"** → Differentiate from other quotes. Offer free sketch to create investment.
